Biography

Hirohito Kondo conducted research on the nature of working memory and attentional control while a PhD student. He obtained his PhD (Experimental Psychology) from Kyoto University in 2002 and spent one year as a postdoctoral fellow. His interest in conscious awareness has led him to investigate where and how meaningful perceptual objects are formed in the human brain. He took up a Research Scientist at NTT Communication Science Laboratories in 2003. He was also a Visiting Scholar, United Graduate School of Child Development, Osaka University from 2014 to 2016. He served as a Lead Guest Editor of the Philosophical Transactions of the Royal Society B in 2016. He came to Chukyo University at Nagoya in 2017, where he is currently a Professor at School of Psychology.

Research Interests

A major goal of my research is to understand the neural mechanisms that enable people to meaningful perceptual objects from ambiguous sensory inputs. To this aim, I use combinations of various approaches, such as psychophysical, neuroimaging, and genotyping methods. My particular focus concerns individual differences in auditory and visual perceptual organization.
